WEEK 12 - PROGRESS

Concept

During the weekly meeting, Paul was able to show considerable progress on Yogi wireframes to team members. Seeing the functionality of the app and the visuals helped to really drill down the concept around how the app would be original, entertaining and essential to users. (IMAGE 5 – WIREFRAMES)

We decided the app would move away from the core idea of facilitating the service of connecting dog walkers to dogs and owners.

We decided that the main theme of the app is foremost a fun interactive social media platform for dogs – instead of their owners (as per Instagram etc). Users would construct profiles for their dogs, using tags and locations, and being able to connect with other profiles as ‘friends’ in-app. We decided to stick with tags for fun identifiers instead of rating pets to foster a friendly community mood on the app between users.

We decided there would be no messaging between users for privacy and protection.

We decided to drill down into the core concept of developing a community for dogs and their owners with the use of geo-locations via Google maps for finding local parks, and services would be targeted for which area users were currently it.

We decided that the local area services would function like pages on Facebook, paying fees for advertising and could be rated by users like in Google reviews.

The core project idea moved away from connecting a service between users to being more about building a community within the app between users, instead of a focus on selling a service we wanted to provide a fun platform in which users can seek services but that is not the main purpose. The focus is on the dogs and their profiles and how this might translate to community meet ups and park visits in the real world.
